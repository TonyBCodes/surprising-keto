Shopping guide

Where to shop

You don't need specialty stores to cook this way. Here's how to find what you need wherever you are.

Advertisement

Warehouse / bulk club

The best value for proteins, olive oil, eggs, dairy, nuts, and pantry staples. Buy in bulk and freeze proteins you won't use within a few days.

Examples: Costco, Sam's Club, BJ's Wholesale, PriceSmart (Caribbean/Latin America)

Search: "warehouse club near me" or "Costco near me"

Standard grocery

Your everyday supermarket handles most of what you need — fresh produce, herbs, condiments, spices, and basic proteins.

Examples: Kroger, Safeway, Publix, Albertsons, Wegmans, Meijer, HEB, and regional chains

Natural or specialty grocery

Best for no-sugar-added condiments (ketchup, BBQ sauce, marinara), high-quality olive oil, clean broths, specialty cheeses, and products with cleaner ingredient lists.

Examples: Whole Foods, Sprouts, Natural Grocers, Fresh Market, Earth Fare

Asian grocery

The best source for fresh whole fish, shellfish, specialty produce, fish sauce, and many pantry items at excellent prices. Worth finding one near you — the fish quality and variety is typically superior to standard grocery.

Examples: H Mart, 99 Ranch, Seafood City, Viet Hoa, and many independent stores

Search: "Asian grocery near me" or "Korean grocery near me" or "Vietnamese grocery near me"

Fish market / fishmonger

For the freshest possible fish, bought same day as cooking. Often less expensive than grocery stores for whole fish, and staff can clean and fillet for you.

Search: "fish market near me" or "fishmonger near me"

Specialty butcher

For premium or specialty cuts — whole lamb shoulder, specific beef cuts, heritage pork. Often better quality than grocery store counters for weekend cooking projects.

Search: "butcher near me" or "specialty meat market near me"

Online

For specialty items, sweeteners, specific keto products, and supplements. Shipped to your door — often cheaper than retail for specific items.

Good for: Monk fruit / allulose sweeteners (Besti brand), no-sugar ketchup, specialty spice blends, quality bone broth, keto-specific supplements

A note on label reading

Some ingredients require careful label checking regardless of where you buy them. Watch for yeast extract, autolyzed yeast, and hydrolyzed protein in broths, condiments, cured meats, and pre-mixed seasonings. These are common hidden ingredients that some people react to.

Recipes on this site flag any ingredient that commonly contains yeast extract so you know what to check.
